Doctrines and Principles

Doctrines
Principles
A doctrine is a fundamental, unchanging truth of the gospel of Jesus Christ. Such truths as Heavenly Father has a body of flesh and bones, baptism is necessary to enter the kingdom of God and all men will be resurrected are examples of doctrines.
A principle is an enduring truth or rule individuals can adopt to guide them in making decisions. Gospel principles are universal and help people apply the doctrines of the gospel to everyday living. Some examples of principles are: praying always can help us overcome temptation (see D&C 10:5). A good practive is to do the following:
  • "If I... then..."
  • "I should... so that..."

Study Tools

  • Ask Questions: Prophets have a message and audience in mind as they write. Ask questions such as: "What is the lesson they want me to understand?" "Why is this important for me today?" and "How can this bless my life?"

  • Word Definitions: Prophets used words familiar to them, but not always to us. Use the Bible Dictionary, or regular dictionary to define words and recognize synonyms. (Example: define the word flaxen in 2 Nephi 26:22)

  • Name Substitution: Insert your name for one in the scriptures. (Example: 1 Nephi 20:14, 16)

  • Scripture Clustering: Group, link, or cross-reference scriptures together to clarify meaning and unlock understanding.

  • Cause and Effect: Loof for if/then and because/therefore relationships. (Example: Jeremiah 7:5-7)

  • Repeated Words: Words and phrases like behold, wherefore, because, nevertheless, or thus we see are invitations to stop and look for meaning. (Example: Helaman 6:35-36; 3 Nephi 18:30-32)

  • Scripture Lists: Prophets often gave lists of warnings and challenges without identifying them. awhen you find a list number each element.

  • Determine the Setting: Define the who, what, when, and where of scripture events and blocks. (Example: Luke 10:25-29 gives the setting for Luke 10:30-37)

  • Visualize: Look for descriptive details and different points of view while creating a mental picture as you read. Ask questions about the events and imagine being present when it took place.

  • Keep a Record: Write down impressions you receive as you read. The Holy Ghost will tell you in your heart what you need to do as you read the scriptures.
